**Reset your iPhone – Quick FAQ**

1. **Backup first**  
   • Go to **Settings > [Your Name] > iCloud > iCloud Backup** and tap **Back Up Now** (or use iTunes/Finder on a computer).

2. **Open Reset menu**  
   • Open **Settings**  
   • Tap **General**  
   • Scroll to the bottom and tap **Transfer or Reset iPhone**.

3. **Choose a reset option**  
   | Option | What it does | When to use |
   |--------|--------------|-------------|
   | **Reset All Settings** | Restores system defaults (Wi‑Fi passwords, wallpaper, etc.) but keeps your data. | If apps or features are misbehaving. |
   | **Erase All Content & Settings** | Wipes the device clean, installing a fresh iOS install. | If you’re selling, gifting, or want a completely fresh start. |
   | **Reset Network Settings** | Clears Wi‑Fi passwords, VPN, etc. | Wi‑Fi or connectivity problems. |
   | **Reset Keyboard Dictionary** | Removes custom words you added. | Keyboard issues. |

4. **Confirm**  
   • Enter your passcode or Apple ID password when prompted.  
   • Read the warning and tap **Erase iPhone** or **Reset All Settings**.

5. **Wait for the process**  
   • Your iPhone will reboot and show the Apple logo.  
   • If you chose “Erase All Content & Settings,” you’ll see the setup screen to restore from backup or set up as new.

**Need a quick restart?**  
- Hold the side button + volume up (or down) until the slider appears, slide to power off, then turn the phone back on.  

That’s it! If you run into any trouble, feel free to ask for help.
